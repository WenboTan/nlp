Here's information about the DAT110 course:

*   **Course Code:** DAT110
*   **Title:** Methods for electronic system design and verification
*   **Credits:** 7.5
*   **Language:** English
*   **Schedule Block:** D+
*   **URL:** https://www.chalmers.se/en/education/your-studies/find-course-and-programme-syllabi/course-syllabus/DAT110/?acYear=2025%2F2026

**What you will learn (Learning Outcomes):**
After completing the course, you should be able to:
*   Describe the algorithmic principles of important Electronic Design Automation (EDA) concepts, such as behavioral and logic synthesis, logic simulation, static timing analysis, timing closure, and power dissipation analysis.
*   Describe contemporary EDA design flows and their fundamental weaknesses and strengths.
*   Apply Linux-based EDA tools, including command scripts, for the design and verification of digital electronic systems.
*   Perform timing-driven synthesis and power dissipation analysis for digital systems.

**Prerequisites:**
*   **Course-specific prerequisites:** Introduction to integrated circuit design (MCC093).
*   **General entry requirements:** Master's level (second cycle).
*   **Specific entry requirements:** English 6 (or equivalent proficiency level).
*   Applicants enrolled in a Chalmers program where the course is included are exempted from fulfilling the above requirements.
